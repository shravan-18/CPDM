Abstract

Positron Emission Tomography (PET) and Computed Tomography (CT) are crucial tools in the diagnosis, staging, and monitoring of numerous diseases, particularly cancer. Despite their significance, the adoption of PET/CT systems in medical facilities is restricted by the need for radioactive material and the limited availability of PET scanners, in contrast to the widespread availability of CT scanners. In this study, we concentrate on a practically significant yet often overlooked issue known as CT to PET image translation. This task involves leveraging deep learning models to generate PET images from CT images. We propose a method named CPDM (CT-to-PET Diffusion Model), which, to the best of our knowledge, is one of the initial attempts to employ a diffusion model for translating from CT to PET images. CPDM utilizes a Brownian Bridge process-based diffusion model to directly learn the translation process from the CT domain to the PET domain, thereby reducing the stochasticity typically encountered in generative models. Moreover, CPDM leverages domain knowledge to create two conditional maps, namely the attention map and the attenuation map, which guide the diffusion process. The attention map assists the model in focusing on potential regions of interest, while the attenuation map enhances PET data correction and ensures precise diagnostic information. We develop a comprehensive dataset consisting of 15,000 paired CT and PET images to support the development and evaluation of the proposed method. Experimental assessments conducted on various benchmarks showcase that CPDM exhibits superior performance across multiple metrics, encompassing both PET quality and training time complexity.
